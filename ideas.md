# Furniture Sales Training Game - Feature Ideas

This document tracks ideas for enhancing the web-based furniture sales training game, inspired by gamification concepts.

## Current Features
- **Level 1: Product Knowledge Quiz** - Multiple-choice questions on products, services, systems.
- **Level 2: Objection Handling Role-Play** - Interactive scenarios with customer objections and response feedback.
- **Level 3: Full Sales Simulation** - Timed challenges with objections, customer management, and goals.

## Potential New Features/Ideas

### 1. Product Knowledge Trivia
- Add points, badges, and leaderboards for quiz performance.
- Include multimedia (images/videos) of products.
- Track progress with performance graphs.

### 2. Interactive Role-Play Dialogues
- Expand branching conversations with more customer types (e.g., budget-conscious, demanding).
- Add voice-over or text animations for immersion.
- Score based on response quality and customer satisfaction.

### 3. Simulation Challenges
- Mini-games like "Inventory Allocation" or "Customer Queue Management".
- Time pressure with penalties for delays.
- Realistic outcomes based on decisions (e.g., stockouts affect sales).

### 4. Progress Visualizations
- Avatars that level up with achievements.
- Progress bars for completing modules or levels.
- Unlockable content (e.g., advanced products after basics).

### 5. Team Competitions
- Multiplayer modes for collaborative sales challenges.
- Leaderboards for individual or team scores.
- Virtual teams with shared goals.

### 6. Reward Systems
- Virtual currency earned from tasks.
- Redeem for in-game perks (e.g., hints) or real rewards (certificates).
- Achievement badges for milestones.

### 7. Story-Driven Narratives
- Frame the game as a salesperson's career progression.
- Narrative arcs tying levels to real-world sales journeys.
- Character backstories for customers.

### 8. Objection Handling Puzzles
- Drag-and-drop mechanics to match objections with responses.
- Puzzle variations (e.g., matching games, word puzzles).
- Difficulty levels with more complex scenarios.

### 9. CRM Tool Simulations
- Gamified tutorials on using CRM software.
- Points for correct data entry, lead tracking, or report generation.
- Simulate real CRM interfaces.

### 10. Achievement Unlocks
- Unlock new levels, products, or features after completing challenges.
- Easter eggs or bonus content for high performers.
- Replayability with new unlocks.

## Implementation Notes
- Prioritize web-friendly features (HTML5, CSS, JS).
- Ensure educational value: Focus on teaching sales skills, product knowledge, and customer service.
- Test for engagement: Use feedback loops, clear goals, and rewards.
- Accessibility: Make sure features are inclusive (e.g., keyboard navigation, screen reader support).

## Future Considerations
- Analytics: Track user progress and effectiveness for training metrics.
- Customization: Allow trainers to add company-specific products or scenarios.
- Mobile Optimization: Ensure responsiveness for on-the-go training.
